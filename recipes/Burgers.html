Description:

Burgers, a most famous and loved food in the states. Perfect for parties, fine cuisine, or sporting events. These loved burgers are said to be and America cuisine, but in reality they too don't originate from the United States. They where actually born in Hamburg, Germany.

Throughout the years multiple variations and kinds of burgers have risen across the country. Some have eggs that melt over the burger others are lettuce wrapped? what a crime. Regardless of variation and type burgers are a staple in American cuisine.

On this page of my recipe website I'm going to teach you how to make a hamburger. nice and simple.

If for whatever reason you want to research the history of a burger(weird) go here

Ingredients you will need:

  • A mix of freshly ground, high grade-beef chuck in an 80/20 mix
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Buns (of your choosing)

Steps:

  1. Start by Mixing your 80/20 mix in a small tray. Remember not to apply too much pressure or you'll end up with tough patties

  2. Season your meat with salt and pepper

  3. Now split your meat into equal patties. Grab about 5 to 6 ounces of meat and roll it into a ball.

  4. Place your ball onto a metal tray and lightly press it down. Your patties should be about 3/4 to an inch thick and about the width of whatever bun you are using.

  5. Now onto grilling the burgers. Preheat your grill, add a bit of canola oil to the grill. Once the oil starts to smoke your grill is ready to cook

  6. Once the grill is ready, season the outside of the patty on last time then add them to the grill. For juicier burgers Do not press down.

  7. Let 5 minutes go by, then flip the burgers.

  8. The time taken to cook the burgers is based on personal preference:

    • Medium Rare - 7 minutes or until internal temperature is 135 degrees F

    • Medium - 8 minutes or until internal temperature is 145 degrees F

    • Well done - 9 minutes or until internal temperature is 150 degrees F
